Equality in Sports



Sports

Sports are considered an essential tool for positive changes for the human being, improving confidence, autonomy, self-improvement and self-esteem. All these elements effectively contribute to its empowerment.

Problems

These effects can vary, or even not be positive if applied incorrectly. Depending on the type of physical preparation, contrary elements such as female irresistence or impotence, for example rugby practice, can be induced or reinforced.

Korfball and Gender Equality

Korfball, from its origins, has always had a neutral perspective, advocated and defended the participation of both genders. This sport, unlike the others, has as its main rule: female and male inclusion, so the teams are mixed.

Moralities of Korfball

This sport has as its principles non-violence, mixed game, cooperation, alternation of functions, techniques and co-education. Due to the blocking of other players during the game is done by gender, that is, men only block men and women only block women, the traditional sporting advantages such as strength, height, speed are weakened, creating a balance in the preformances.

Originality

All these elements are found only in this sport. It encourages and encourages the breaking of the traditional stereotype: men with men and women with women.


Info Korfball









Rules

1º You cannot take the ball out of the hands of the opponent or a teammate;
2º You can't run, walk or dribble with the ball;
3º You cannot throw the ball from a defensive position;
4º At the beginning of the game, after each basket scored, the ball is played from the center line (in the middle of it);
5º Each time the ball is introduced into the basket, it is equivalent to a point...


Dimensions and Formations of/in the Field

- The entire field measures 40X20 meters and is divided in half by a line;
- Each zone has a basket placed 3.5 meters from the baseline;
- There is a penalty mark 2.5m in front of the basket;
- In each of the zones, 4 players and 4 players from each team are placed, called “square”: in one zone there will be an attacking square and in the other a defensive square;
- The games only last 6 minutes.


Game Court

- The dimensions of the playing court are 40 x 20 m;
- The playing field is divided into 2 equal zones by a line parallel to the middle line (width direction).


The Game Field

- The playing field must be marked by clearly visible lines between 3 and 5 cm wide.
- The center parting line is marked in the same way.
- Penalty points must be scored 2.5 m from the post on the longitudinal axis and towards the center of the field.
- The penalty area can be marked on the field, around each post.
- These areas can be defined by a color contrasting with the lines and the playing field, or by fixed lines on the field, showing the boundaries of the area.


Netless Basket

- The basket must be oriented towards the center of the field and its upper edge must be 3.5m above ground level.
- The baskets are cylindrical without a bottom.


Referees

- The referee controls the game. His tasks are as follows:

- Decide on the suitability of the field, and material;
- The referee makes sure before the match, that the conditions of the field of play are adequate; that the material meets the stipulated conditions and that everything is ready for the start of the game.
- Pay attention to any changes that occur during the game.
